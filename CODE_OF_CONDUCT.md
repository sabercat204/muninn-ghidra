# Code of Conduct

Do no evil.
Do no harm.
Use freely.
Be responsible.

That's it. The hacker register, distilled.

If you need elaboration: this is reverse-engineering tooling. It exists to help you understand binaries you have the right to understand — your own software, software you've been authorized to audit, public CTF challenges, your team's release artifacts. Don't use it to attack systems you don't own. Don't use it to ship vulnerability findings into the wild without giving the maintainers a chance to fix things first. Don't use it to harm people.

When in doubt: would the person whose binary you're reading recognize what you're doing as a legitimate use, or would they recognize it as an attack on them? If the second, stop.

The freedom to take things apart and learn how they work is one of the foundations of the craft. It comes with the responsibility to not weaponize that learning against people who didn't ask for it. The community that built reverse engineering as a discipline already knows this. If you're new: welcome, and learn the norms.
